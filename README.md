## 1. Introduction
THEE Coffee Table is a multicultural women’s empowerment group formed during the COVID-19 pandemic. Founded on 7 July 2021, the organisation began with three women gathering around a small coffee table and has since grown into a movement impacting more than 400–500 women across Victoria, with expansion now beginning in South Australia.

THEE Coffee Table exists to uplift, empower, train, and equip women—especially those facing isolation, trauma, domestic violence, financial hardship, or cultural barriers—to discover their inner potential (“the gold mine within”) and step confidently into their future.

---

## 2. Historical Background

### 2.1 Origins
During lockdown, many women experienced:
- Isolation and loneliness  
- Mental health challenges  
- Domestic violence  
- Loss of income  
- Loss of confidence and social connection  

To respond to this need, the founder established a safe space for women to talk, share, and support one another.

### 2.2 Early Growth
- Week 1: 3 women  
- Week 2: 7 women  
- Week 3: 9 women  
- Week 4: Lockdown resumed; meetings moved to Zoom  
- Zoom attendance grew to 20–35 participants weekly  

### 2.3 Support & Partnerships
With support from the local council and Manor Lakes Community Centre, women were connected to:
- Mental health services  
- Medical professionals  
- Job opportunities  
- Food relief  
- Essential household support

## 3. Programs & Community Impact

### 3.1 Weekly Support Groups
- Senior coffee group  
- Single mothers’ support  
- Multicultural chat & networking  
- Youth engagement  
- Women’s empowerment circles  

### 3.2 Skills & Training Programs
THEE Coffee Table offers training in:
- Business start-up (SME program)  
- Cooking and sustainable meal planning  
- Art, craft, floristry, paper-making  
- Potting and backyard gardening  
- Barista and hospitality skills  
- Sewing, fashion design, and creative arts  
- Settlement training for migrants and asylum seekers  
- Household management (Western + cultural integration)  
- Confidence-building and public speaking  

Many participants have launched their own businesses (art classes, sewing, home baking, beauty services, floristry, catering).

### 3.3 Community Kitchen (2022)
- Founded with $2,000 personal investment  
- Provided free breakfasts weekly to 40–50 families  
- Supported the homeless  
- Offered training in hospitality and cooking  
- Connected with local food bank operations  

### 3.4 Community Garden
Established with support from:
- Bunnings (materials)  
- Council grant of $1,000  

Benefits include therapy, skill-building, community connection, and food production.
